# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

> **Note:** This document has been expanded to address process improvements identified in [Issue #4](https://github.com/NihadZeidan/skills-scale-institutional-knowledge-using-copilot-spaces/issues/4), adding comprehensive definitions for Agile Coach, QA Lead, Business Analyst, and UX/UI Designer roles.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Agile Coaches facilitate agile ceremonies, mentor teams on agile practices and principles, and help remove organizational impediments. They foster a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint ceremonies (planning, daily standups, reviews, retrospectives)
- Coach team members on agile methodologies and best practices
- Identify and help remove organizational blockers and impediments
- Guide teams in self-organization and cross-functional collaboration
- Support the adoption of agile metrics and continuous improvement
- Mentor Project Managers and Scrum Masters on advanced facilitation techniques

### Goals
- Increase team velocity and predictability over time
- Build sustainable agile practices and mindsets
- Reduce waste and improve flow efficiency
- Foster psychological safety and team empowerment

### Typical Communication
- Attend and facilitate sprint ceremonies
- One-on-one coaching sessions with team members
- Retrospective facilitation and action item tracking
- Collaborate with Project Managers on process improvements

### Workflow Interactions
- **With Project Managers:** Partners to improve team processes, ceremony effectiveness, and impediment removal
- **With Developers:** Coaches on technical practices (TDD, pair programming, continuous integration)
- **With Product Managers:** Helps align product backlog refinement with agile principles
- **With QA Lead:** Supports integration of quality practices into agile workflows

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads own the quality assurance strategy, test coverage, and defect management. They ensure that quality is built into the development process and that releases meet acceptance criteria.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain QA strategy and testing standards
- Oversee test planning, execution, and automation efforts
- Manage defect triage, prioritization, and resolution tracking
- Establish quality gates and Definition of Done criteria
- Coordinate manual and automated testing efforts
- Review acceptance criteria and provide quality feedback during planning
- Report on quality metrics and test coverage

### Goals
- Ensure high product quality and minimal production defects
- Increase test automation coverage and efficiency
- Reduce time to detect and resolve defects
- Build quality practices into the development lifecycle

### Typical Communication
- Daily standup participation for quality status updates
- Test plan reviews with developers and product teams
- Defect triage meetings and severity assessments
- Quality metrics reporting in sprint reviews

### Workflow Interactions
- **With Developers:** Collaborates on test strategy, reviews code quality, and provides early feedback on testability
- **With Project Managers:** Provides quality status, test progress, and risk assessments for release decisions
- **With Product Managers:** Validates acceptance criteria and ensures user stories are testable
- **With Business Analysts:** Reviews requirements for completeness and testability

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts elicit, document, and manage requirements, acting as a bridge between stakeholders and the development team. They ensure that business needs are clearly understood and translated into actionable user stories.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct stakeholder interviews and requirements gathering sessions
- Document business requirements, use cases, and user stories
- Translate business needs into technical requirements
- Facilitate requirement clarification and acceptance criteria definition
- Validate solutions against business objectives
- Maintain requirements traceability and documentation
- Support user acceptance testing (UAT) coordination

### Goals
- Ensure complete and clear requirements that align with business objectives
- Minimize rework due to unclear or missing requirements
- Bridge communication gaps between business and technical teams
- Improve stakeholder satisfaction through accurate solution delivery

### Typical Communication
- Requirements review sessions with stakeholders
- User story refinement with Product Managers and development team
- Acceptance criteria walkthroughs
- UAT planning and results communication

### Workflow Interactions
- **With Product Managers:** Collaborates to refine product backlog, clarify user stories, and define acceptance criteria
- **With Developers:** Answers requirement questions, provides business context, and validates implemented solutions
- **With UX/UI Designers:** Ensures designs meet business requirements and user needs
- **With QA Lead:** Collaborates on test scenarios and UAT planning
- **With Stakeholders:** Elicits needs, validates solutions, and manages expectations

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers create user experiences and interfaces that align with project goals, brand standards, and user needs. They advocate for the end-user throughout the development process.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Design user interfaces following design systems and accessibility standards
- Collaborate with developers on design implementation
- Validate designs through user feedback and analytics
- Maintain design documentation and component libraries
- Ensure consistency in user experience across products

### Goals
- Create intuitive, accessible, and delightful user experiences
- Reduce user friction and improve task completion rates
- Ensure design consistency and adherence to brand guidelines
- Validate designs with real user feedback

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with stakeholders and team members
- Usability testing sessions and findings presentations
- Collaboration sessions with developers on implementation
- Design critiques and feedback sessions

### Workflow Interactions
- **With Product Managers:** Collaborates on feature definition, user needs validation, and prioritization
- **With Business Analysts:** Ensures designs meet business requirements and user scenarios
- **With Developers:** Partners on design implementation, provides assets, and validates UI accuracy
- **With QA Lead:** Defines UI acceptance criteria and participates in visual regression testing
- **With Stakeholders:** Presents designs, gathers feedback, and validates solutions
